Should I Repair or Replace My HVAC Unit?

When your heating or air conditioning unit stops working, you may need to decide whether to repair it or replace it. Use this interactive tool to help you make the best choice for your situation.

Is the repair under warranty?

If your unit is under warranty without labor included, a portion of the cost is covered. If your warranty has expired, consider the age of your system as the type and cost of the repair rises.

No Yes

How old is your system?

If your HVAC unit is old, warranty will have expired or soon will. Typical service life is approximately 15–20 years for furnaces, 10–15 years for air conditioners, 10–15 years for heat pumps.

Less than 10 years 10-15 years More than 15 years

What is the estimated cost of repair?

A service or repair might cost you typically around hundreds, but may be a range of larger problems. If a major repair is needed, replacing the unit may be a better choice.

Covered under warranty Less than $1,000 More than $1,000

Have frequent repairs been needed?

Frequent repairs in the same year or over the life of a system indicates the unit may not be reliable and replacement or upgrade may be necessary.

No Yes

Have you noticed declining performance?

A change in your home’s comfort, like uneven temperatures, increased humidity, or an unexplained increase in energy bills are signs of aging equipment that may not be meeting the demands of your needs.

No Yes

Do you plan to stay in your current home for at least five more years?

If you plan to move soon, repair is a good option because you likely won’t benefit from the system’s energy savings or long-term value. If you plan to stay in your home, replacement may be a better choice.

No Yes

Get your recommendation:

Find your points total in the ranges below to get your repair vs replace recommendation.

0–4 Repair
If your system is under warranty or requires a minor, low-cost repair, fixing the system is recommended to quickly restore service. However, remember that older systems often have more issues over time. Talk with your HVAC technician about the condition of your unit.
5–8 Replace
If your system is out of warranty, near or past its lifespan, requires a major repair, or shows signs of declining performance, you should replace it. A new unit helps make your home more comfortable, saves energy, and gives you additional features. It also comes with a new warranty.
